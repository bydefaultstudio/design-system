We started with a mission to create a workplace that works for our brains — not one that boxes us in.

Both our co-founders are neurodivergent and are building a creative workplace that's neuro-affirmative and supportive. We have an in-house ADHD practitioner who helps creatives unlock their gifts or find strategies to organise and communicate their thinking.

At the age of 11, one of our co-founders, Erlen Masson, was told by a teacher he'd never amount to anything in life.

All because he couldn't read or spell like his classmates.

Then he found Photoshop, and discovered a new way to communicate to the world.

He became an award-winning visual storyteller not despite his dyslexia, but *because of it*.

This story isn't rare. It's just rarely recognised.

> "It's estimated that between 20–50% of creative professionals identify as neurodivergent, compared to about 15% in the general UK population. While neurodiversity brings unique strengths like innovative thinking and diverse perspectives that can be highly beneficial in the creative workplace, research indicates that these individuals are often underserved and face specific challenges."

By Default was created to centre the minds that fuel the creative industry but are too often unsupported, misunderstood, or pushed to burnout.

We're building a different kind of creative workplace — one where we don't need to mask.

One that flexes to fit the support needs of how we think, work and communicate.

One where inclusive design, accessibility and culture is our default standard.

For most of our lives, we've been told "you don't belong here" — in classrooms, in workplaces, in media that weren't designed with us in mind. This experience drives our core purpose: to create truly inclusive destinations where all kinds of thinkers can thrive, to let our creative work speak for itself, and to leave the world a little better than we found it.
