Most design systems are too big. They were too big the day they shipped, they keep getting bigger every quarter, and the maintainers are slowly drowning in a Slack channel called #ds-questions. The answer to almost every one of those questions is "yes, that exists, but it lives at a path nobody can guess."

A small design system is a strong one. A handful of tokens, a handful of layout primitives, a handful of components — and rules for what counts as "core" versus "one-off." The work isn't building more. The work is saying no.

This article will eventually argue for a maximum-thirty-component cap and walk through the kinds of components that almost always belong in that thirty (button, input, card, callout, dialog) and the kinds that almost never do (anything with the word "Hero" in its name).
